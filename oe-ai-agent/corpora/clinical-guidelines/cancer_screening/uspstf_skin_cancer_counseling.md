# Skin Cancer Prevention: Behavioral Counseling

## Recommendation Summary

| Population | Recommendation | Grade |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Young adults, adolescents, children, and parents of young children (ages 6 months to 24 years) with fair skin types | Counsel about minimizing exposure to ultraviolet (UV) radiation to reduce risk of skin cancer. | B |
| Adults older than 24 years with fair skin types | Selectively offer counseling to adults older than 24 years with fair skin types about minimizing UV radiation exposure to reduce risk of skin cancer. The likelihood of benefit is small. | C |

## Population

This recommendation applies to persons aged 6 months to 24 years with fair skin types who are at increased risk for skin cancer.

**Fair skin type characteristics** (Fitzpatrick types I and II):
- Always or usually burns
- Tans poorly
- Often has light skin, light hair, freckles, light eye color

## Counseling Components

Effective behavioral counseling interventions address minimizing UV exposure through:

- **Avoiding intentional sun exposure** during peak intensity hours (10 AM to 4 PM)
- **Wearing sun-protective clothing** (long sleeves, long pants, wide-brimmed hats)
- **Wearing sunglasses** that block UV rays
- **Seeking shade** when outdoors
- **Using broad-spectrum sunscreen** with SPF 15 or higher (most experts recommend SPF 30+); applying generously and reapplying every 2 hours and after swimming or sweating
- **Avoiding indoor tanning beds**

## Counseling Delivery

Effective interventions varied in format:
- Print materials, digital interventions, or in-person counseling
- Single sessions or multi-session interventions
- Tailored interventions based on individual risk and skin type

## Importance

Skin cancer is the most commonly diagnosed cancer in the US. Melanoma is the most lethal form. UV radiation exposure (from sun and tanning beds) is the most important modifiable risk factor for skin cancer.

## Related USPSTF Recommendations

The USPSTF separately concluded that current evidence is insufficient to assess the balance of benefits and harms of visual skin examination by a clinician to screen for skin cancer in adults (I statement).
