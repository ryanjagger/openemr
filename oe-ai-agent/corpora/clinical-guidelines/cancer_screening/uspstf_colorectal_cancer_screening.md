# Colorectal Cancer: Screening

## Recommendation Summary

| Population | Recommendation | Grade |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Adults aged 50 to 75 years | Screen for colorectal cancer in all adults. | A |
| Adults aged 45 to 49 years | Screen for colorectal cancer. | B |
| Adults aged 76 to 85 years | Selectively offer screening. The net benefit of screening all persons in this age group is small. Consider patient's overall health, prior screening history, and preferences. | C |

## Population

Adults 45 years and older who do not have signs or symptoms of colorectal cancer and who are at **average risk** for colorectal cancer.

Average risk = no prior diagnosis of colorectal cancer, adenomatous polyps, or inflammatory bowel disease; no personal diagnosis or family history of known genetic disorders that predispose to high lifetime risk of colorectal cancer (such as Lynch syndrome or familial adenomatous polyposis).

## Recommended Screening Strategies

| Test | Frequency |
| --- | --- |
| High-sensitivity guaiac fecal occult blood test (HSgFOBT) | Every year |
| Fecal immunochemical test (FIT) | Every year |
| Stool DNA-FIT | Every 1 to 3 years |
| Computed tomography colonography | Every 5 years |
| Flexible sigmoidoscopy | Every 5 years |
| Flexible sigmoidoscopy + annual FIT | Sigmoidoscopy every 10 years + FIT yearly |
| Colonoscopy | Every 10 years |

Clinicians and patients may consider a variety of factors in deciding which test may be best for each person. The tests differ in:
- Frequency of screening required
- Location of screening (home or office)
- Methods of screening (stool-based or direct visualization)
- Preprocedure bowel preparation requirements
- Anesthesia or sedation during the test
- Follow-up procedures for abnormal findings (positive stool tests require colonoscopy)

## Implementation Notes

**For adults aged 76 to 85 years:** Discuss together with patients the decision to screen, taking into consideration:
- Patient's overall health status (life expectancy, comorbid conditions)
- Prior screening history
- Patient preferences

## What's New in This Recommendation

The USPSTF expanded the recommended ages for colorectal cancer screening to 45 to 75 years (previously, it was 50 to 75 years). The USPSTF continues to recommend selectively screening adults aged 76 to 85 years for colorectal cancer.
