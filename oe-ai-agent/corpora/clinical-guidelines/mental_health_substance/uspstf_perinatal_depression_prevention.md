# Perinatal Depression: Preventive Interventions

## Recommendation Summary

| Population | Recommendation | Grade |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Pregnant and postpartum persons at increased risk of perinatal depression | Provide or refer to counseling interventions. | B |

## Definition

Perinatal depression refers to a major or minor depressive episode during pregnancy or in the first 12 months following delivery.

## Population

This recommendation applies to pregnant and postpartum persons at increased risk for perinatal depression who are not currently depressed and have not been diagnosed with a depressive disorder.

## Identifying Persons at Increased Risk

Risk factors for perinatal depression include:
- **History of depression** (personal history or current depressive symptoms)
- **History of physical or sexual abuse**
- **Adolescent or single parenthood**
- **Unplanned or unwanted pregnancy**
- **Recent intimate partner violence**
- **Mental health symptoms (anxiety, mood lability)**
- **Adverse life events**
- **Low socioeconomic status**
- **Low social support**
- **Having a complicated pregnancy**

There is no formal accurate risk-prediction tool. A pragmatic approach is to refer pregnant or postpartum persons with one or more of these risk factors to counseling.

## Effective Counseling Interventions

Two types of counseling shown to be effective:

1. **Cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT)** — most extensively studied
   - Addresses thought patterns and behaviors that contribute to depression
   - Typically delivered in group settings or individual sessions
   - Sessions during pregnancy and/or postpartum

2. **Interpersonal therapy (IPT)**
   - Addresses interpersonal relationships and life transitions
   - Particularly suited to the perinatal period

### Common features of effective interventions:
- Multiple sessions (median 8 sessions)
- Delivered by trained interventionists (mental health professionals, nurses, midwives)
- May begin in pregnancy or postpartum
- Group or individual format

## Effectiveness

Counseling interventions reduced the risk of perinatal depression by approximately 39% (pooled risk ratio 0.61).

## Importance

Perinatal depression affects approximately 8.5-11% of pregnant and postpartum persons in the US. Untreated perinatal depression is associated with:
- Adverse pregnancy outcomes (preterm birth, low birth weight)
- Impaired parent-infant bonding
- Adverse infant developmental outcomes
- Increased risk of postpartum suicide
- Increased risk of maternal substance use

## Related USPSTF Recommendations

- Screening for depression and suicide risk in adults (including pregnant and postpartum persons)
- Healthy weight and weight gain during pregnancy
- Hypertensive disorders of pregnancy screening
