# Anxiety in Children and Adolescents: Screening

## Recommendation Summary

| Population | Recommendation | Grade |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Children and adolescents aged 8 to 18 years | Screen for anxiety. | B |
| Children 7 years or younger | Current evidence is insufficient to assess the balance of benefits and harms of screening. | I |

## Population

This recommendation applies to children and adolescents aged 8 to 18 years who do not have a diagnosed mental health disorder and are not showing recognizable signs or symptoms of anxiety.

## Screening Tools

Validated screening tools for children and adolescents include:

- **Screen for Child Anxiety Related Disorders (SCARED)** — child- and parent-report versions
- **Spence Children's Anxiety Scale (SCAS)**
- **Multidimensional Anxiety Scale for Children (MASC)**
- **Generalized Anxiety Disorder 7-item (GAD-7)** — for older adolescents
- **Patient Health Questionnaire for Adolescents (PHQ-A)**
- **Pediatric Symptom Checklist (PSC)**

Tools are typically self-report (and/or parent-report for younger children); the choice depends on age, language, and clinical setting.

## Common Anxiety Disorders in Children and Adolescents

- Generalized anxiety disorder (GAD)
- Social anxiety disorder
- Separation anxiety disorder
- Specific phobias
- Panic disorder
- Selective mutism
- Obsessive-compulsive disorder (related but classified separately)

## Implementation

- Screen during well-child visits, behavioral health visits, or as part of routine care
- Have referral pathways established before screening
- Follow positive screens with diagnostic evaluation (clinical interview)
- Treatment may include cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT), pharmacotherapy (SSRIs as first-line), or both depending on severity

## Effective Treatments

For diagnosed anxiety disorders in children and adolescents:

### Psychotherapy:
- **Cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT)** — most evidence-based intervention
- Often delivered in individual or group format
- Family-based CBT for younger children
- Exposure-based therapies for specific anxiety disorders

### Pharmacotherapy:
- **SSRIs** are first-line pharmacologic treatment
- FDA-approved options for anxiety in children/adolescents include sertraline, fluoxetine, fluvoxamine
- Consider when symptoms are severe or psychotherapy alone is insufficient
- Combined treatment (CBT + SSRI) often superior to either alone for moderate-severe anxiety

### Important considerations:
- All antidepressants carry FDA black-box warning about increased suicidal ideation in children/adolescents
- Close monitoring during initiation and dose changes
- Family involvement in treatment is important

## Importance

Anxiety disorders are common in children and adolescents:
- Prevalence: approximately 7-11% of children and adolescents
- Often underrecognized and undertreated
- Untreated childhood anxiety can lead to:
  - Academic problems
  - Social difficulties
  - Impaired family functioning
  - Increased risk of depression and substance use disorders
  - Persistence into adulthood (if untreated)
