# Intimate Partner Violence and Caregiver Abuse of Older or Vulnerable Adults: Screening

## Recommendation Summary

| Population | Recommendation | Grade |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Women of reproductive age, including pregnant and postpartum women | Screen for intimate partner violence (IPV). | B |
| Older or vulnerable adults | Current evidence is insufficient to assess the balance of benefits and harms of screening for caregiver abuse of older or vulnerable adults. | I |

## Population

This recommendation applies to women of reproductive age (typically 14 to 46 years), including those who are pregnant or postpartum.

## Definition

Intimate partner violence (IPV) is abuse or aggression that occurs in a romantic relationship and includes:
- Physical violence
- Sexual violence
- Stalking
- Psychological aggression (including coercive acts)
- Reproductive coercion (e.g., interfering with contraception)

## Screening Tools

Several validated screening tools have acceptable accuracy:

- **HITS** (Hurt, Insult, Threaten, Scream) — 4 items
- **OVAT** (Ongoing Violence Assessment Tool)
- **STaT** (Slapped, Threatened, and Throw)
- **HARK** (Humiliate, Afraid, Rape, Kick)
- **CTQ-SF** (Childhood Trauma Questionnaire - Short Form, when adapted)
- **WAST** (Woman Abuse Screening Tool)

Screening is typically administered as a written questionnaire or via direct verbal questioning by the clinician.

## Implementation

When screening for IPV:
- Ensure privacy and confidentiality (the patient should be alone, not with a partner or family member)
- Use trauma-informed approaches
- Be aware of mandatory reporting requirements in your jurisdiction
- Have a referral pathway in place before screening (e.g., social work, advocacy organizations, hotlines)

When screening is positive, clinicians should provide or refer to:
- **Ongoing supportive care** (validation, acknowledgment, safety planning)
- **Local IPV advocacy resources**
- **Mental health support** (counseling, trauma-focused therapy)
- **Legal resources** (e.g., protective orders)
- **Safety planning resources**

National resources include:
- The National Domestic Violence Hotline (1-800-799-7233)
- StrongHearts Native Helpline (1-844-7NATIVE)
- The National Sexual Assault Hotline (1-800-656-HOPE)

## Importance

IPV is highly prevalent: approximately 1 in 3 women in the US experience IPV at some point in their lifetime. IPV is associated with:
- Acute physical injuries
- Chronic pain conditions
- Depression and anxiety
- PTSD
- Substance use disorders
- Adverse pregnancy outcomes
- Homicide (intimate partner homicide is a leading cause of death for pregnant and postpartum women)
- Long-term cardiovascular and metabolic effects

## Effective Interventions

Effective interventions for women who screen positive include:
- Counseling interventions (especially during pregnancy)
- Home visitation programs
- Brief in-clinic interventions including referral and resources

## Related USPSTF Recommendations

- Depression and suicide risk screening in adults
- Anxiety screening in adults
- Unhealthy alcohol use screening
