# Recommended Adult Immunization Schedule

## Overview

The CDC's Advisory Committee on Immunization Practices (ACIP) develops recommendations on the use of vaccines for adults aged 19 years and older. The schedule is updated annually.

This document summarizes routine adult vaccinations. Always check CDC's current schedule for definitive guidance, as recommendations change.

**Important context (as of 2026):** Because of disruption to the federal ACIP/CDC immunization recommendation process in 2025, the American Academy of Pediatrics, American Academy of Family Physicians, and a coalition of specialty societies have published independent immunization schedules that may diverge from the CDC schedule on certain vaccines. For a clinician-facing system, ingest both and flag conflicts.

## Routine Adult Vaccinations

### Influenza (Flu)
- **Recommended:** All persons 6 months and older annually
- **Form:** Standard-dose, high-dose, or adjuvanted vaccines available
- **For adults 65+:** Higher-dose or adjuvanted vaccines preferentially recommended

### Tdap / Td (Tetanus, Diphtheria, +/- Pertussis)
- **Tdap once** (if not previously received), then **Td or Tdap booster every 10 years**
- **During each pregnancy:** Tdap during 27-36 weeks gestation (regardless of prior vaccination)
- **Wound management:** Tdap (preferred over Td) if last dose >5 years ago

### MMR (Measles, Mumps, Rubella)
- **Born ≥1957:** Document evidence of immunity (1 or 2 doses depending on risk group, or laboratory evidence)
- **Born before 1957:** Generally considered immune from natural infection
- **Healthcare workers, students, international travelers:** 2 doses required

### Varicella
- **Adults without immunity:** 2-dose series (4-8 weeks apart)
- **Born before 1980:** Generally considered immune (except healthcare workers and pregnant women, who need lab evidence)

### HPV (Human Papillomavirus)
- **Routine for adults through age 26** (catch-up vaccination)
- **Shared clinical decision-making for adults aged 27-45** based on individual risk

### Zoster (Shingles)
- **Recombinant zoster vaccine (Shingrix):** 2-dose series for adults ≥50 years
- **Adults ≥19 years who are immunocompromised:** 2-dose series

### Pneumococcal
- **All adults aged 50+:**
  - PCV20 (single dose), OR
  - PCV15 followed by PPSV23 (≥1 year later)
- **Adults 19-49 with certain risk conditions:** Same options
- Risk conditions include chronic heart, lung, liver, kidney disease; diabetes; cigarette smoking; alcoholism; cochlear implants; CSF leaks; immunocompromising conditions

### Hepatitis B
- **Universally recommended for adults 19-59 years** if not previously vaccinated
- **Adults ≥60 with risk factors** for HBV: vaccinate
- **Adults ≥60 without risk factors:** May vaccinate (shared decision-making)
- **Series:** 2-, 3-, or 4-dose schedule depending on vaccine product

### Hepatitis A
- **Adults at risk** (international travelers, MSM, persons who inject drugs, persons experiencing homelessness, persons with chronic liver disease)
- **2-dose series** at least 6 months apart

### Meningococcal (MenACWY, MenB)
- **MenACWY for college freshmen living in residence halls** if not previously vaccinated
- **MenACWY and/or MenB for adults at risk** (asplenia, complement deficiency, HIV, microbiologists, military recruits)

### COVID-19
- Recommendations have evolved frequently. Refer to current CDC and ACIP guidance.
- Generally: annual updated COVID-19 vaccine recommended for high-risk groups
- Dosing schedules and product recommendations change with each annual update

### RSV (Respiratory Syncytial Virus)
- **Adults ≥75 years:** Single dose recommended
- **Adults 50-74 years at increased risk for severe RSV disease:** Single dose
- **Pregnant persons at 32-36 weeks gestation:** Single dose during September-January (to protect infant)

### Mpox
- **2-dose series for adults at risk** for Mpox (e.g., MSM with multiple recent partners, sex workers, persons with recent STI diagnosis)

## Vaccines Recommended Based on Conditions or Indications

Persons with certain medical conditions, occupations, lifestyles, or travel plans may need additional vaccines or modified schedules. Examples:

- **Pregnancy:** Tdap, influenza, RSV, COVID-19; live vaccines (MMR, varicella, LAIV) generally avoided
- **Immunocompromised states:** Live vaccines generally avoided; may need additional doses of inactivated vaccines
- **Healthcare workers:** Hepatitis B, MMR, varicella, Tdap, annual influenza
- **Travel:** Hepatitis A, typhoid, yellow fever, Japanese encephalitis, rabies, others depending on destination

## Catch-up Vaccinations

Adults who missed routine childhood vaccines should receive them per the catch-up schedule, including:
- MMR (1 or 2 doses)
- Varicella (2 doses)
- HPV (through age 26)
- Hepatitis B (3-dose series)
- Tdap and Td

## Implementation Notes

- Confirm vaccination status using state immunization information systems (IIS) when possible
- Any contraindicated vaccine should be deferred; document the reason
- Vaccination during a minor illness (without fever) is generally acceptable
- Use Vaccine Information Statements (VIS) for informed consent
