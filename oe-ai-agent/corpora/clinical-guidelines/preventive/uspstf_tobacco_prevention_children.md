# Tobacco and Nicotine Use Prevention in Children and Adolescents: Primary Care Interventions

## Recommendation Summary

| Population | Recommendation | Grade |
| --- | --- | --- |
| School-aged children and adolescents who have not started to use tobacco | Provide interventions, including education or brief counseling, to prevent initiation of tobacco use. | B |
| School-aged children and adolescents who use tobacco | Current evidence is insufficient to assess the balance of benefits and harms of primary care interventions for cessation of tobacco use. | I |

## Population

This recommendation applies to children and adolescents (typically ages 5-18) who have not yet started using tobacco products, including:
- Cigarettes
- Cigars
- Smokeless tobacco
- Hookah
- Electronic cigarettes (e-cigarettes/vaping products)
- Other emerging tobacco products

## Effective Interventions

The USPSTF identified that **education or brief counseling** in primary care can reduce initiation of tobacco use.

### Common features of effective interventions:
- **Education** about the harms of tobacco/nicotine use
- **Brief counseling** during pediatric visits
- **Multiple modalities** including:
  - Face-to-face counseling
  - Print materials (handouts, brochures)
  - Phone or computer-based interventions
  - Combinations of the above

### Specific approaches:
- Discussions with the child/adolescent and parents/caregivers
- Anticipatory guidance during well-child visits
- Targeting age-appropriate messages (developmentally tailored)
- Addressing peer influences and social norms
- Addressing family tobacco use (parents, siblings)

### Counseling content:
- Risks of tobacco/nicotine use (immediate and long-term)
- Addiction potential
- Specific concerns about e-cigarettes/vaping (which the USPSTF includes in this recommendation)
- Strategies for refusing/avoiding tobacco
- Involvement of family in supporting tobacco-free environment

## E-Cigarettes and Vaping

The USPSTF recommendation explicitly includes prevention of e-cigarette and vaping product use. Specific concerns:

- High nicotine content in many products (especially "pod" devices)
- Strong addiction potential
- Health harms (including E-cigarette or Vaping product use Associated Lung Injury [EVALI])
- Use in adolescents has been a public health concern
- Use of e-cigarettes is associated with subsequent initiation of combustible tobacco

## Implementation

Pediatric clinicians can:
- **At every well-child visit:**
  - Ask about tobacco/nicotine use (and use by family members and peers)
  - Assess knowledge of risks
  - Provide age-appropriate counseling and education
- **Document** counseling in the medical record
- **Provide handout/educational materials** for the family
- **For households with smokers:**
  - Encourage smoking cessation in caregivers
  - Recommend smoke-free home and vehicle
  - Discuss reduction in secondhand smoke exposure

## Related Considerations

### Secondhand smoke exposure:
- Document secondhand smoke exposure
- Counsel on harms (asthma exacerbation, otitis media, SIDS)
- Assist parents/caregivers in cessation

### Children of tobacco users:
- Higher likelihood of starting tobacco use
- More intensive counseling may be warranted

## Importance

- Most adult tobacco users start before age 18
- Adolescent brain particularly susceptible to nicotine addiction
- Tobacco use is the leading cause of preventable death in the US
- Tobacco-related death is largely preventable through prevention of initiation
- E-cigarette use has been a significant and evolving issue in adolescents

## Related USPSTF Recommendations

- Tobacco smoking cessation in adults, including pregnant persons
- Lung cancer screening
- Behavioral counseling for cardiovascular disease prevention
