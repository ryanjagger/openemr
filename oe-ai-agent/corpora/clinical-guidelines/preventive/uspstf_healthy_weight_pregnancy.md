# Healthy Weight and Weight Gain in Pregnancy: Behavioral Counseling Interventions

## Recommendation Summary

| Population | Recommendation | Grade |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Pregnant persons | Offer effective behavioral counseling interventions aimed at promoting healthy weight gain and preventing excess gestational weight gain in pregnancy. | B |

## Population

This recommendation applies to all pregnant persons.

## IOM Recommended Weight Gain in Pregnancy (Single Pregnancy)

Based on prepregnancy BMI:

| Prepregnancy BMI | Recommended Total Weight Gain | Rate of Weight Gain (2nd & 3rd Trimesters) |
|---|---|---|
| Underweight (<18.5) | 28-40 lb (12.5-18 kg) | ~1 lb/week |
| Normal weight (18.5-24.9) | 25-35 lb (11.5-16 kg) | ~1 lb/week |
| Overweight (25.0-29.9) | 15-25 lb (7-11.5 kg) | ~0.6 lb/week |
| Obesity (≥30) | 11-20 lb (5-9 kg) | ~0.5 lb/week |

For twin pregnancies, recommendations are higher; refer to ACOG/IOM specific guidance.

## Effective Behavioral Counseling Interventions

The USPSTF identified effective interventions had these characteristics:
- **Multicomponent approach** addressing diet, physical activity, and behavior change
- **Multiple sessions** (typically ≥4-12 sessions) throughout pregnancy
- **Counseling delivered by:** dietitians, nurses, midwives, exercise physiologists, or trained interventionists
- **Format:** in-person individual or group sessions; some interventions used technology (text messages, web-based)
- **Median total contact time:** 6 hours over multiple sessions

### Common counseling components:
- Goal-setting (specific weight gain targets)
- Self-monitoring of weight, food intake, and physical activity
- Education on healthy eating in pregnancy
- Education on physical activity in pregnancy (targeting ≥150 min/week of moderate activity, when not contraindicated)
- Behavior change strategies
- Motivational interviewing

## Healthy Eating in Pregnancy

Recommendations include:
- Adequate caloric intake (additional ~340 kcal/day in 2nd trimester, ~450 kcal/day in 3rd trimester for normal weight pregnancies)
- Balanced macronutrients
- Adequate protein, calcium, iron, folate
- Folic acid supplementation
- Iron supplementation as indicated
- Avoid: alcohol, raw/undercooked meats and fish, unpasteurized dairy, high-mercury fish

## Physical Activity in Pregnancy

When not contraindicated:
- 150 minutes per week of moderate-intensity aerobic activity
- Includes activities like brisk walking, swimming, prenatal yoga, stationary cycling
- Avoid contact sports, activities with fall risk, scuba diving, hot yoga
- Strength training generally safe

Contraindications to exercise in pregnancy include:
- Hemodynamically significant heart disease
- Restrictive lung disease
- Incompetent cervix
- Multiple gestation at risk for premature labor
- Placenta previa after 26 weeks
- Premature labor
- Ruptured membranes
- Preeclampsia/pregnancy-induced hypertension
- Severe anemia

## Effectiveness

Behavioral interventions in pregnancy have been shown to:
- Reduce excess gestational weight gain
- Reduce risk of cesarean delivery
- Reduce risk of macrosomia
- Reduce gestational diabetes (in some studies)

## Importance

Excess gestational weight gain is associated with:
- Postpartum weight retention
- Long-term obesity in mother
- Macrosomia and large-for-gestational-age infants
- Cesarean delivery
- Gestational diabetes
- Hypertensive disorders of pregnancy
- Childhood obesity in offspring

Inadequate weight gain is associated with:
- Small-for-gestational-age infants
- Preterm birth

## Related USPSTF Recommendations

- Gestational diabetes screening
- Hypertensive disorders of pregnancy screening
- Aspirin for preeclampsia prevention
- Folic acid supplementation
- Smoking cessation in pregnancy
- Perinatal depression preventive interventions
