# Falls Prevention in Community-Dwelling Older Adults: Interventions

## Recommendation Summary

| Population | Recommendation | Grade |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Community-dwelling adults 65 years or older at increased risk for falls | Recommend exercise interventions to prevent falls. | B |
| Community-dwelling adults 65 years or older at increased risk for falls | Selectively offer multifactorial interventions to prevent falls. The evidence indicates that the overall net benefit of routinely offering multifactorial interventions to prevent falls is small. | C |

## Population

This recommendation applies to community-dwelling adults 65 years or older who are at increased risk for falls but who are not known to have osteoporosis or vitamin D deficiency.

## Identifying Patients at Increased Risk for Falls

Risk factors include:
- History of falls (one or more falls in the past year)
- History of mobility problems
- Poor performance on tests of balance and gait (e.g., Timed Up and Go, 30-second chair stand)
- Older age (75+)
- Female sex
- Lower-extremity weakness
- Cognitive impairment
- Polypharmacy (especially psychoactive medications)
- Chronic conditions (Parkinson's disease, stroke, arthritis, vision impairment)
- Home environmental hazards

Tools to identify increased risk include:
- The Timed Up and Go test
- Self-reported falls in the past year
- Self-reported mobility problems

## Effective Exercise Interventions

Effective exercise interventions identified in the evidence reviews:
- **Group classes** that include gait, balance, and functional training
- **Individually tailored exercise programs** delivered at home or in a community setting
- **Tai Chi** programs
- **Multi-component exercise** programs combining several types of training

Common features of effective programs:
- Emphasize balance, gait, and functional training
- Often include resistance/strengthening components
- Sessions typically 1-3 times per week
- Total intervention duration of several months to 1+ year
- Programs supervised by trained instructors

## Multifactorial Interventions

Multifactorial interventions involve initial assessment for individual risk factors, followed by individualized interventions targeting those risk factors. Components may include:
- Comprehensive geriatric assessment
- Medication review and adjustment (especially psychoactives)
- Vision assessment
- Cognitive assessment
- Cardiovascular assessment (orthostatic hypotension)
- Home environment assessment
- Footwear assessment
- Vitamin D assessment

Multifactorial interventions have small net benefit for the average person; they may be appropriate for those with the highest risk profiles.

## Vitamin D Supplementation

The USPSTF has separately concluded that vitamin D supplementation does NOT prevent falls in community-dwelling adults 65 years or older without known osteoporosis or vitamin D deficiency (D recommendation).
