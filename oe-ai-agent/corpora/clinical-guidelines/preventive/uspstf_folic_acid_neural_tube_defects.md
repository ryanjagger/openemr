# Folic Acid Supplementation to Prevent Neural Tube Defects: Preventive Medication

## Recommendation Summary

| Population | Recommendation | Grade |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Persons who are planning to or could become pregnant | Take a daily supplement containing 0.4 to 0.8 mg (400 to 800 mcg) of folic acid. | A |

## Population

This recommendation applies to all persons who are planning to or could become pregnant, including:
- Persons actively trying to conceive
- Persons of reproductive age who could become pregnant (whether planned or not)
- Adolescents and adults capable of becoming pregnant

## Implementation

- **Dose:** 0.4 to 0.8 mg (400 to 800 mcg) of folic acid daily
- **Timing:** Begin before conception and continue through the first trimester (at least)
- **Source:** Daily supplement (multivitamin or dedicated folic acid supplement)

Most prenatal vitamins contain 0.4-1.0 mg of folic acid, which meets this recommendation.

## Higher-Dose Folic Acid for High-Risk Persons

Higher doses of folic acid (typically 4 mg/day) are recommended for persons at increased risk of having a child with neural tube defects, including:
- Previous pregnancy affected by a neural tube defect
- Personal history of a neural tube defect
- Partner with a neural tube defect

This higher dose is typically prescribed beginning 1-3 months before conception and continued through the first trimester. Persons in this group should consult their healthcare provider for individualized recommendations.

## Effectiveness

Folic acid supplementation reduces the risk of neural tube defects by approximately 50-70%. Neural tube defects include:
- Spina bifida
- Anencephaly
- Encephalocele

Neural tube formation is complete by about 28 days after conception, often before pregnancy is recognized. This is why supplementation must begin BEFORE conception.

## Public Health Context

Since 1998, the US has fortified enriched cereal grain products with folic acid. This has reduced neural tube defect rates by approximately 35%. However, fortification alone does not provide sufficient folic acid for many persons of reproductive age, which is why supplementation is recommended.

## Importance

Neural tube defects are among the most common serious birth defects, affecting approximately 3,000 pregnancies in the US annually. They can cause permanent disability or death.
