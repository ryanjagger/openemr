# High Body Mass Index in Children and Adolescents: Interventions

## Recommendation Summary

| Population | Recommendation | Grade |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Children and adolescents 6 years or older with high BMI (≥95th percentile for age and sex) | Provide or refer to comprehensive, intensive behavioral interventions. | B |

## Population

Children and adolescents 6 years or older with high BMI (≥95th percentile for age and sex on CDC or WHO growth charts).

## Definition of "High BMI"

The USPSTF uses the term "high BMI" rather than "obesity" to describe children with a BMI at or above the 95th percentile for age and sex. This terminology recognizes that BMI is a screening measure, not a diagnostic test.

## Comprehensive, Intensive Behavioral Interventions

Effective interventions identified in the evidence reviews include:

### Key features:
- **Total contact time:** ≥26 hours over 2-12 months
- **Multicomponent approach:**
  - Physical activity
  - Healthy eating
  - Behavior change techniques
  - Family involvement
- **Multiple sessions:** typically 26 or more hours of intervention
- **Group, individual, or mixed delivery formats**
- **Trained interventionists:** including dietitians, exercise physiologists, behavior therapists, physicians, nurses

### Common behavior change techniques:
- Goal setting
- Self-monitoring
- Stimulus control
- Problem solving
- Family-based approaches
- Skill-building (e.g., reading nutrition labels, meal planning)

### Setting:
- Primary care
- Schools
- Community settings (including YMCA programs)
- Specialized weight management clinics

## Effectiveness

Comprehensive, intensive behavioral interventions in children and adolescents 6 years or older with high BMI led to:
- Modest reductions in BMI z-score and BMI percentile at 6-12 months
- Improvements in cardiometabolic outcomes (blood pressure, lipids, fasting glucose)
- Improvements in physical activity and dietary intake
- Improvements in quality of life

## Pharmacotherapy

The USPSTF noted limited evidence on the role of FDA-approved weight loss medications in children and adolescents (such as orlistat, phentermine/topiramate ER, liraglutide, semaglutide, setmelanotide for specific genetic causes). The recommendation focuses on behavioral interventions as the primary approach.

The American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP) 2023 guidelines recommend offering evidence-based pharmacologic options as adjuncts to behavioral interventions for children 12 years or older with obesity. Refer to the AAP Clinical Practice Guideline for the Evaluation and Treatment of Children and Adolescents With Obesity for more detailed pharmacologic guidance.

## Bariatric Surgery

The USPSTF did not address bariatric surgery in this recommendation. AAP 2023 guidelines state that adolescents 13 years or older with severe obesity (BMI ≥120% of 95th percentile or ≥35 kg/m²) may be evaluated for bariatric surgery in expert referral centers.

## Importance

In the US, approximately 19.7% of children and adolescents aged 2-19 years have obesity (high BMI). Childhood obesity is associated with:
- Type 2 diabetes
- Hypertension
- Dyslipidemia
- Non-alcoholic fatty liver disease
- Obstructive sleep apnea
- Mental health conditions
- Increased risk of obesity persisting into adulthood
- Earlier onset of cardiovascular disease
