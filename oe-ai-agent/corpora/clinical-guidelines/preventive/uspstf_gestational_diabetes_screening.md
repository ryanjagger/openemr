# Gestational Diabetes: Screening

## Recommendation Summary

| Population | Recommendation | Grade |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Asymptomatic pregnant persons at 24 weeks of gestation or after | Screen for gestational diabetes. | B |
| Asymptomatic pregnant persons before 24 weeks of gestation | Current evidence is insufficient to assess the balance of benefits and harms of screening for gestational diabetes mellitus before 24 weeks. | I |

## Definition

Gestational diabetes mellitus (GDM) is defined as glucose intolerance with onset or first recognition during pregnancy.

## Screening Tests

Two strategies are commonly used in the US for screening pregnant persons at 24-28 weeks of gestation:

### Two-step approach (most commonly used in the US):
1. **Step 1:** 50-g oral glucose challenge test (GCT) — non-fasting
   - If 1-hour glucose ≥130 mg/dL (or ≥140 mg/dL, depending on threshold used), proceed to step 2
2. **Step 2:** 100-g oral glucose tolerance test (OGTT) — fasting
   - GDM diagnosis if 2 or more of the following are met:
     - Fasting: ≥95 mg/dL
     - 1 hour: ≥180 mg/dL
     - 2 hour: ≥155 mg/dL
     - 3 hour: ≥140 mg/dL

### One-step approach (used internationally):
- 75-g OGTT — fasting
- GDM diagnosis if any of the following:
  - Fasting: ≥92 mg/dL
  - 1 hour: ≥180 mg/dL
  - 2 hour: ≥153 mg/dL

## Risk Factors for GDM

- Obesity (BMI ≥30)
- Prior history of GDM or macrosomic infant
- First-degree relative with diabetes
- Polycystic ovary syndrome
- Belonging to an ethnic group with higher risk (Asian American, African American, Hispanic, Pacific Islander, Native American)
- Maternal age ≥35
- Multifetal pregnancy

## Treatment

If GDM is diagnosed, management generally includes:
1. **Medical nutrition therapy** with a registered dietitian
2. **Self-monitoring of blood glucose** (typically fasting and postprandial)
3. **Physical activity** as appropriate
4. **Pharmacologic therapy** if glucose targets are not met with lifestyle alone:
   - Insulin (preferred and most studied)
   - Oral agents (metformin, glyburide) — used in some practices but with caveats

## Postpartum Follow-up

Persons with GDM should be screened for type 2 diabetes 4-12 weeks postpartum (with a 75-g OGTT or fasting plasma glucose), and then every 1-3 years thereafter, as they have a substantially increased risk of type 2 diabetes (50% lifetime risk).

## Importance

GDM affects approximately 6-9% of pregnancies in the US. Untreated GDM is associated with:
- Increased risk of macrosomia and shoulder dystocia
- Increased risk of cesarean delivery
- Increased risk of preeclampsia
- Increased risk of neonatal hypoglycemia and hyperbilirubinemia
- Increased lifetime risk of type 2 diabetes for the mother
- Increased risk of obesity and type 2 diabetes for the offspring
