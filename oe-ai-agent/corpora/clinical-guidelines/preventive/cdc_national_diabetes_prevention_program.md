# National Diabetes Prevention Program (National DPP)

## Overview

The National Diabetes Prevention Program (National DPP) is a CDC-led partnership that delivers an evidence-based lifestyle change program to prevent or delay type 2 diabetes in adults with prediabetes or at high risk for diabetes. The program is based on the Diabetes Prevention Program (DPP) randomized controlled trial, which demonstrated that lifestyle change reduced the development of type 2 diabetes by 58% (and by 71% in adults ≥60 years).

## Eligibility

Adults are eligible for a CDC-recognized National DPP lifestyle change program if they:
- Are at least 18 years old
- Have a BMI ≥25 kg/m² (≥23 kg/m² for Asian Americans)
- Are NOT currently pregnant
- Have NOT been previously diagnosed with type 1 or type 2 diabetes
- Have a qualifying risk factor:
  - **Blood test result** within the previous year showing prediabetes:
    - Fasting plasma glucose 100-125 mg/dL, OR
    - HbA1c 5.7%-6.4%, OR
    - 2-hour postload glucose 140-199 mg/dL
  - **OR a clinical diagnosis** of gestational diabetes during a previous pregnancy
  - **OR a high score on the CDC/ADA Prediabetes Risk Test** (≥5)

## Program Structure

The CDC-recognized lifestyle change program is a structured, year-long program with:

**Year 1:**
- 16 sessions in the first 6 months (typically weekly), then 6 monthly sessions in the second 6 months
- Total of approximately 22 sessions over 12 months
- Delivered by a trained lifestyle coach
- Group-based (typically 8-15 participants), in-person or online

**Curriculum focuses on:**
- Healthy eating
- Increasing physical activity (goal: 150 minutes per week of moderate activity)
- Stress management
- Behavior change strategies (problem-solving, goal-setting, self-monitoring)
- Cognitive-behavioral approaches to weight loss

**Goals:**
- 5-7% weight loss
- 150 minutes per week of moderate physical activity

## Effectiveness

The original DPP trial (referenced repeatedly in clinical guidelines):
- Lifestyle intervention reduced type 2 diabetes incidence by 58% over 3 years
- 71% reduction in adults ≥60 years
- Long-term follow-up (Diabetes Prevention Program Outcomes Study) showed continued benefit
- Effective across racial and ethnic groups, sexes, and BMI categories

Real-world National DPP data:
- Average weight loss in participants completing the program: 4-6%
- Reduction in diabetes incidence consistent with research findings

## Coverage

**Medicare:** The Medicare Diabetes Prevention Program (MDPP) is a covered benefit for eligible Medicare beneficiaries since 2018. Includes:
- 16 core sessions over 6 months
- 6 monthly maintenance sessions
- Up to 12 months of additional ongoing maintenance for those who maintain weight loss goal
- No cost-sharing for beneficiaries

**Medicaid:** Coverage varies by state. Many states cover the National DPP through Medicaid.

**Commercial insurance:** Many insurers cover the program; varies by plan.

**Self-pay:** Programs may also be available to individuals paying out of pocket; costs vary.

## Finding a Program

Clinicians and patients can find local CDC-recognized programs through:
- **CDC National DPP Registry:** https://nccd.cdc.gov/DDT_DPRP/Programs.aspx
- The registry includes in-person and online programs
- Filterable by location, language, delivery format, and CDC recognition status

## Referral Process

For clinicians:
1. **Identify patients with prediabetes** through screening (per USPSTF guidelines, screen adults 35-70 with overweight/obesity)
2. **Communicate the diagnosis** of prediabetes to the patient
3. **Discuss lifestyle change as the most effective intervention**
4. **Provide a referral or recommendation** to a CDC-recognized National DPP program
5. **Use the prediabetes/diabetes prevention referral tools** available through professional society websites and EHRs

Many EHRs now include built-in referral pathways to local DPP programs.

## Why Refer Rather Than Counsel?

The National DPP delivers a level of intensity (~22 sessions over 12 months) that is difficult to provide in primary care visits. Brief in-office counseling alone has not demonstrated equivalent effectiveness. The structured group-based, year-long program provides:
- Sustained behavioral support
- Peer accountability
- Trained coaching
- Evidence-based curriculum

## Pharmacologic Alternative

For patients not enrolling in or completing the National DPP:
- **Metformin** can be considered for diabetes prevention in adults with prediabetes who are at high risk (per ADA guidance)
- Metformin is most effective in: BMI ≥35, persons younger than 60 years, women with prior gestational diabetes
- Metformin is not FDA-approved for this indication but is widely used
