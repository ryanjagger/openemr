# Hepatitis B Virus Infection in Adolescents and Adults: Screening

## Recommendation Summary

| Population | Recommendation | Grade |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Adolescents and adults at increased risk for HBV infection | Screen for hepatitis B virus (HBV) infection in adolescents and adults at increased risk. | B |

## Population at Increased Risk

Adolescents and adults at increased risk for HBV infection include:

- Persons born in countries or regions with a prevalence of HBV infection of 2% or greater
- US-born persons not vaccinated as infants whose parents were born in regions with HBV prevalence of 8% or greater (including most of Asia, sub-Saharan Africa, and parts of Eastern Europe)
- Persons with HIV-positive status
- Persons who inject drugs (past or current)
- Men who have sex with men
- Sex partners and household contacts of persons with HBV infection
- Patients receiving hemodialysis
- Persons with elevated serum aminotransferase levels of unknown etiology

## Screening Tests

Screening for HBV infection is performed using a 3-test panel (or as a stepwise approach):
1. **Hepatitis B surface antigen (HBsAg)** — detects current HBV infection
2. **Antibody to hepatitis B surface antigen (anti-HBs)** — indicates immunity
3. **Antibody to hepatitis B core antigen (total anti-HBc)** — indicates past or current infection

## Periodic Screening

Persons who initially test negative but remain at increased risk should be screened periodically. The optimal interval is unknown.

## Treatment and Management

Persons identified with chronic HBV infection (HBsAg-positive ≥6 months) should be:
- Referred to clinicians experienced in managing HBV infection
- Evaluated for antiviral therapy
- Monitored for hepatocellular carcinoma per appropriate guidelines

Effective antiviral therapies for chronic HBV infection include:
- Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate
- Tenofovir alafenamide
- Entecavir
- Pegylated interferon (selected patients)

## Vaccination

Vaccination remains the most effective way to prevent HBV infection. Hepatitis B vaccination is recommended universally in infancy as part of the routine immunization schedule. Adults aged 19-59 years should receive hepatitis B vaccination if not previously vaccinated.
