# CDC Sexually Transmitted Infections (STI) Treatment Guidelines

## Overview

The CDC's STI Treatment Guidelines provide evidence-based recommendations for the diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of sexually transmitted infections in the United States. These are widely considered the authoritative US guidance for STI care.

This document summarizes recommendations for the most common STIs encountered in primary care. Always check current CDC guidance, as antimicrobial resistance patterns evolve and recommendations are updated.

## Chlamydia trachomatis

**Diagnosis:**
- Nucleic acid amplification testing (NAAT) on urogenital, rectal, or pharyngeal specimens
- Self-collected vaginal swabs are equivalent to clinician-collected swabs

**Recommended treatment (uncomplicated genital infection):**
- **Doxycycline** 100 mg orally twice daily for 7 days

**Alternative:**
- **Azithromycin** 1 g orally as a single dose
- **Levofloxacin** 500 mg orally once daily for 7 days

**Treatment in pregnancy:**
- **Azithromycin** 1 g orally as a single dose

**Important notes:**
- Treat sex partners from the past 60 days
- Test of cure not routinely recommended for uncomplicated infection (except in pregnancy)
- Retest in 3 months due to high reinfection rates

## Neisseria gonorrhoeae

**Diagnosis:**
- NAAT on urogenital, rectal, or pharyngeal specimens

**Recommended treatment (uncomplicated infection of cervix, urethra, rectum):**
- **Ceftriaxone** 500 mg IM as a single dose (1 g IM if weight ≥150 kg)
- If chlamydia infection has not been excluded: add **doxycycline** 100 mg PO BID × 7 days (or azithromycin 1 g PO if pregnant)

**Pharyngeal infection:**
- Same as above; ceftriaxone is essential due to lower cure rates of alternatives at pharyngeal sites

**Penicillin/cephalosporin allergy:**
- **Gentamicin** 240 mg IM PLUS **azithromycin** 2 g PO (single dose each)

**Important notes:**
- Treat sex partners from the past 60 days
- Test of cure for pharyngeal gonorrhea recommended (NAAT 7-14 days after treatment)
- Retest at 3 months for all sites due to high reinfection rates
- Increasing antimicrobial resistance is a major concern; refer to current CDC guidance

## Syphilis

**Diagnosis (serologic):**
- **Traditional algorithm:** Nontreponemal test (RPR or VDRL) → confirm with treponemal test (FTA-ABS or TP-PA)
- **Reverse algorithm:** Treponemal immunoassay (EIA or CIA) → confirm with nontreponemal test
- Direct testing (darkfield microscopy or PCR) for primary lesions

**Treatment by stage:**

**Primary, secondary, or early latent syphilis (≤1 year):**
- **Benzathine penicillin G** 2.4 million units IM as a single dose

**Late latent syphilis (>1 year, unknown duration, or tertiary):**
- **Benzathine penicillin G** 2.4 million units IM weekly × 3 doses (total 7.2 million units)

**Neurosyphilis or ocular syphilis or otosyphilis:**
- **Aqueous crystalline penicillin G** 18-24 million units IV daily (3-4 million units every 4 hours, or continuous infusion) × 10-14 days

**Penicillin allergy (non-pregnant):**
- **Doxycycline** 100 mg PO BID × 14 days for early stages, × 28 days for late
- Pregnant patients with penicillin allergy: **desensitize and treat with penicillin**

**Important notes:**
- Jarisch-Herxheimer reaction within 24 hours common, especially in early stages
- Follow-up serology at 6, 12, 24 months to monitor treatment response
- Treat sex partners from the past 90 days (primary), 6 months (secondary), or 12 months (early latent)
- Test for HIV, hepatitis B, hepatitis C; offer HIV PrEP if appropriate

## Trichomoniasis

**Diagnosis:**
- NAAT or culture on vaginal swab or urine
- Wet mount has low sensitivity (40-60%)

**Treatment:**
- **Women:** Metronidazole 500 mg PO BID × 7 days (preferred over single 2 g dose)
- **Men:** Metronidazole 2 g PO as a single dose
- **Alternative:** Tinidazole 2 g PO as a single dose

**Important notes:**
- Treat sex partners
- Patient education on alcohol avoidance during and 24 hours after metronidazole, 72 hours after tinidazole
- Pregnant women with trichomoniasis should be treated; metronidazole is safe in pregnancy

## Genital Herpes (HSV)

**Diagnosis:**
- PCR (preferred) or viral culture from active lesion
- Type-specific serology for chronic infection or asymptomatic patients

**First episode treatment (any of):**
- **Acyclovir** 400 mg PO TID × 7-10 days
- **Valacyclovir** 1 g PO BID × 7-10 days
- **Famciclovir** 250 mg PO TID × 7-10 days

**Recurrent episode treatment (start within 24 hours of symptom onset):**
- **Acyclovir** 800 mg PO TID × 2 days
- **Valacyclovir** 500 mg PO BID × 3 days OR 1 g PO once daily × 5 days
- **Famciclovir** 1 g PO BID × 1 day

**Suppressive therapy (≥6 outbreaks/year, or to reduce transmission):**
- **Acyclovir** 400 mg PO BID
- **Valacyclovir** 500 mg or 1 g PO once daily
- **Famciclovir** 250 mg PO BID

## Pelvic Inflammatory Disease (PID)

**Treatment (outpatient, uncomplicated):**
- **Ceftriaxone** 500 mg IM single dose (1 g if weight ≥150 kg)
- **PLUS Doxycycline** 100 mg PO BID × 14 days
- **PLUS Metronidazole** 500 mg PO BID × 14 days

**Treatment (inpatient, severe PID, tubo-ovarian abscess, pregnancy, etc.):**
- IV regimens; refer to CDC guidance

## Doxycycline Post-Exposure Prophylaxis (Doxy-PEP)

**Recommended (2024 CDC guidance):** Discuss doxy-PEP with men who have sex with men (MSM) and transgender women who:
- Have had a bacterial STI (chlamydia, gonorrhea, syphilis) in the past 12 months
- Are at increased risk for STI acquisition

**Regimen:** Doxycycline 200 mg orally as a single dose within 72 hours after sex (do not exceed 200 mg per 24 hours)

**Effectiveness:** Reduces incidence of chlamydia, syphilis, and (less effectively) gonorrhea

## STI Screening Recommendations Summary

- **HIV:** All adults aged 15-65 years at least once (USPSTF Grade A); annually for high-risk groups
- **Chlamydia/gonorrhea:** All sexually active women ≤24 years annually; women ≥25 with risk factors; sexually active MSM at all sites of exposure
- **Syphilis:** All persons at increased risk; all pregnant women at first prenatal visit
- **Hepatitis B:** All persons at increased risk; all pregnant women
- **Hepatitis C:** All adults ≥18 years at least once; those at continued risk periodically
- **Trichomoniasis:** Women with HIV; consider in women at increased risk

## Reporting

Most STIs are reportable to public health authorities. Confirm reporting requirements in your jurisdiction.
