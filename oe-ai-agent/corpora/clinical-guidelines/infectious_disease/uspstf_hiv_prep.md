# Prevention of HIV Acquisition: Preexposure Prophylaxis (PrEP)

## Recommendation Summary

| Population | Recommendation | Grade |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Adolescents and adults at increased risk of HIV acquisition | Prescribe preexposure prophylaxis using effective antiretroviral therapy to decrease the risk of acquiring HIV. | A |

## Population at Increased Risk

PrEP is recommended for adolescents and adults at increased risk for acquiring HIV. Increased risk includes:

- **Sexually active men who have sex with men (MSM)** who have had a male sexual partner in the past 6 months and any of:
  - A male sexual partner with HIV (especially if not virally suppressed)
  - Inconsistent use of condoms during receptive or insertive anal sex
  - A bacterial sexually transmitted infection (STI) in the past 6 months (syphilis, gonorrhea, or chlamydia)

- **Heterosexually active persons** who have had a sexual partner in the past 6 months and any of:
  - A sexual partner with HIV (especially if not virally suppressed)
  - Inconsistent use of condoms during sex with a partner whose HIV status is unknown and who is at high risk
  - A bacterial STI (syphilis or gonorrhea) in the past 6 months
  - Sex partner who injects drugs

- **Persons who inject drugs** with any of:
  - Injection drug-using partner with HIV
  - Sharing of injection drug equipment
  - Risk of sexual acquisition

- **Persons engaged in transactional sex** (e.g., sex for money, drugs, or housing)

- **Anyone requesting PrEP** even if specific behaviors are not reported, as risk behaviors may not always be disclosed

## Effective Antiretroviral Therapy for PrEP

The FDA has approved 3 medications for PrEP:

1. **Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate/emtricitabine (TDF/FTC)** — daily oral medication, approved for sexually active adults and adolescents weighing at least 35 kg, and for persons who inject drugs
2. **Tenofovir alafenamide/emtricitabine (TAF/FTC)** — daily oral medication, approved for adults and adolescents weighing at least 35 kg at risk of HIV from sex (excluding those at risk from receptive vaginal sex)
3. **Cabotegravir** — long-acting injectable medication administered every 2 months

## Implementation

Before starting PrEP:
- Confirm HIV-negative status with a negative HIV test
- Assess kidney function (TDF/FTC and TAF/FTC) or hepatic function as appropriate
- Test for sexually transmitted infections including syphilis, gonorrhea, chlamydia, and hepatitis B
- Assess for any current acute HIV infection symptoms

While on PrEP:
- HIV testing every 3 months
- STI screening every 3-6 months
- Renal function monitoring (every 6-12 months for TDF/FTC; less frequent for TAF/FTC)
- Counsel on adherence and ongoing risk reduction

## Effectiveness

PrEP is highly effective at reducing HIV acquisition when taken as prescribed:
- TDF/FTC reduces risk of HIV from sex by approximately 99% with high adherence
- Cabotegravir injectable PrEP has shown superior effectiveness compared with oral TDF/FTC in some studies due to adherence advantages
