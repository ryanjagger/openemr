# Syphilis Infection in Nonpregnant Adolescents and Adults: Screening

## Recommendation Summary

| Population | Recommendation | Grade |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Asymptomatic, nonpregnant adolescents and adults at increased risk | Screen for syphilis infection. | A |

(USPSTF also has separate Grade A recommendation for early universal screening in pregnancy.)

## Population at Increased Risk

Adolescents and adults at increased risk for syphilis infection include:

- **Men who have sex with men (MSM)**
- **Persons with HIV infection**
- **Persons taking HIV PrEP**
- **Persons with a history of incarceration**
- **Persons with a history of sex work**
- **Persons in geographic areas with high syphilis prevalence**
- **Persons with sexual partners who are at increased risk** for syphilis (or other STIs)
- **Persons who have a recent diagnosis of another STI** (gonorrhea, chlamydia, HIV)
- **Persons with multiple sexual partners** or whose partners have multiple partners

## Screening Tests

### Traditional Algorithm:
1. Nontreponemal test (RPR or VDRL) as initial screen
2. If positive, confirmatory treponemal test (FTA-ABS, TP-PA, or treponemal EIA/CIA)

### Reverse Sequence Algorithm (used by many laboratories):
1. Treponemal immunoassay (EIA or CIA) as initial screen
2. If positive, confirmatory nontreponemal test (RPR or VDRL)
3. If treponemal positive but nontreponemal negative, perform a second different treponemal test (e.g., TP-PA)

Both algorithms are acceptable; the laboratory typically chooses based on workflow.

## Screening Intervals

Optimal screening intervals are not established.

**Reasonable approach:**
- Annual screening for those at continued increased risk
- More frequent (every 3-6 months) for MSM and others at very high risk
- After exposure to a partner with syphilis: test now and 6 weeks later

## Treatment

See CDC STI Treatment Guidelines (separate document) for current dosing.

**Brief summary:**
- **Primary, secondary, early latent (≤1 year):** Benzathine penicillin G 2.4 million units IM × 1 dose
- **Late latent (>1 year), tertiary:** Benzathine penicillin G 2.4 million units IM weekly × 3 doses
- **Neurosyphilis, ocular, otosyphilis:** IV aqueous penicillin G × 10-14 days
- **Penicillin allergy (non-pregnant):** Doxycycline alternative
- **Penicillin allergy (pregnant):** Desensitize and treat with penicillin

## Importance

Syphilis cases in the US have been increasing rapidly:
- Total cases: more than tripled from 2014 to 2022
- Congenital syphilis: more than 10-fold increase since 2012, reaching epidemic levels
- Highest rates among MSM, but rates rising among all populations
- Untreated syphilis can progress to severe complications including neurosyphilis, cardiovascular syphilis, and death

## Public Health Approach

- All cases must be reported to public health authorities
- Treat sex partners (look-back periods vary by stage)
- Test all patients with positive syphilis screening for HIV, hepatitis B, and hepatitis C
- Consider HIV PrEP if not already prescribed
