# DASH Eating Plan (Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension)

## Overview

The DASH eating plan is an evidence-based dietary pattern developed through NIH-funded research to reduce blood pressure. It is endorsed by the USPSTF, ACC/AHA, and other authoritative bodies as a cornerstone lifestyle intervention for hypertension prevention and management.

## Effectiveness

Original DASH trials showed:
- **Standard DASH diet:** Reduces systolic BP by approximately 5.5 mm Hg and diastolic BP by approximately 3 mm Hg in normotensive adults
- **DASH plus low sodium (1500 mg/day):** Reduces systolic BP by approximately 11 mm Hg in adults with hypertension
- Effects comparable to single-drug therapy in many cases
- Also improves: LDL cholesterol, insulin sensitivity

## Core Principles

The DASH eating plan emphasizes:

### Foods to emphasize:
- **Fruits and vegetables** — 8-10 servings per day
- **Whole grains** — 6-8 servings per day
- **Low-fat or fat-free dairy** — 2-3 servings per day
- **Lean proteins** — fish, poultry, beans, nuts, seeds
- **Plant-based fats** — vegetable oils (especially olive oil), nuts, seeds

### Foods to limit:
- **Sodium** — ≤2300 mg/day standard; 1500 mg/day for greater BP reduction
- **Sweets and added sugars** — ≤5 servings per week
- **Red and processed meats** — limit
- **Saturated fat** — <6% of total calories
- **Trans fat** — avoid
- **Sugar-sweetened beverages** — limit

## Sample Daily Intake (2000-calorie reference)

| Food group | Servings per day | Examples of one serving |
|---|---|---|
| Grains (mostly whole) | 6-8 | 1 slice bread; 1 cup cereal; ½ cup cooked rice/pasta |
| Vegetables | 4-5 | 1 cup raw leafy; ½ cup cooked; ½ cup vegetable juice |
| Fruits | 4-5 | 1 medium fruit; ½ cup fresh/frozen/canned; ¼ cup dried |
| Fat-free or low-fat dairy | 2-3 | 1 cup milk/yogurt; 1.5 oz cheese |
| Lean meat, poultry, fish | ≤6 oz | 1 oz cooked; 1 egg |
| Nuts, seeds, legumes | 4-5 per week | ⅓ cup nuts; 2 tbsp seeds; ½ cup cooked beans |
| Fats and oils | 2-3 | 1 tsp soft margarine; 1 tsp vegetable oil; 1 tbsp mayo |
| Sweets and added sugars | ≤5 per week | 1 tbsp sugar; 1 tbsp jam; 8 oz sugar-sweetened drink |

## Sodium Guidance

- **Standard DASH:** ≤2300 mg sodium per day (1 teaspoon table salt)
- **Lower-sodium DASH:** ≤1500 mg sodium per day (especially recommended for: adults ≥51 years, Black persons, persons with hypertension, diabetes, or chronic kidney disease)

**Sources of sodium to be aware of:**
- Processed and packaged foods (often hidden sodium)
- Restaurant and fast food meals
- Bread and bakery products
- Cured meats and cold cuts
- Canned soups and vegetables
- Cheese
- Sauces, dressings, condiments

## Implementation Tips for Patients

**Gradual changes:**
- Add a serving of vegetables to lunch and dinner
- Use fruit as a snack or dessert
- Switch to whole grain alternatives
- Read sodium labels and choose lower-sodium options
- Limit processed and prepared foods
- Use herbs and spices instead of salt for flavor
- Drink more water; reduce sugary drinks

**Combine with other lifestyle changes:**
- Regular physical activity (150-300 min/week moderate aerobic + 2 days strengthening)
- Weight loss if overweight/obese
- Limit alcohol (≤1 drink/day for women, ≤2/day for men)
- Smoking cessation
- Stress management

## Use in Specific Populations

- **Hypertension:** First-line lifestyle intervention; effective alone or with medication
- **Prehypertension:** Can prevent progression to hypertension
- **Diabetes:** Improves glycemic control and cardiovascular risk
- **CKD:** Generally beneficial; may need modification if advanced CKD (potassium, phosphorus)
- **Pregnancy:** Generally safe and beneficial; consult clinician
- **Heart failure:** Sodium restriction component is important

## Monitoring

For patients adopting DASH for hypertension:
- Recheck BP in 4-6 weeks
- Reassess in 3 months
- Combine with home BP monitoring
- Document lifestyle changes alongside medication adjustments
