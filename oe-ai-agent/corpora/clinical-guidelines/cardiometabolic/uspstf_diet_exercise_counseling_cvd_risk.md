# Healthy Diet and Physical Activity for Cardiovascular Disease Prevention in Adults With Cardiovascular Risk Factors: Behavioral Counseling Interventions

## Recommendation Summary

| Population | Recommendation | Grade |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Adults with cardiovascular disease risk factors | Offer or refer adults with cardiovascular disease risk factors to behavioral counseling interventions to promote a healthy diet and physical activity. | B |

## Population

Adults 18 years or older with one or more of the following:
- Hypertension
- Dyslipidemia
- Mixed or multiple risk factors for cardiovascular disease (e.g., metabolic syndrome)
- Estimated 10-year cardiovascular disease risk of 7.5% or greater

This recommendation does NOT apply to adults with abnormal glucose levels or type 2 diabetes mellitus, who are addressed in a separate USPSTF recommendation. The USPSTF also has a separate recommendation for behavioral counseling for adults without cardiovascular risk factors.

## Components of Effective Interventions

Effective behavioral counseling interventions identified in evidence reviews:
- **Duration:** Most interventions delivered over months (typically 6-12 months)
- **Intensity:** Most studies provided medium- to high-intensity counseling
- **Median time spent in intervention:** Approximately 6 hours over 6-12 months across multiple sessions
- **Components:** Most interventions include both diet and physical activity counseling

### Counseling delivery formats:
- In-person individual counseling
- In-person group counseling
- Telephone counseling
- Print materials (often as supplement)
- Video/web-based platforms

### Most effective interventions:
- Used multiple sessions (median ~12 sessions)
- Combined dietary and physical activity counseling
- Used established behavior change techniques (motivational interviewing, goal setting, self-monitoring, problem solving)
- Were delivered by trained interventionists, including primary care clinicians, nurses, registered dietitians, exercise physiologists, lifestyle coaches, and behavioral therapists

## Healthy Diet Recommendations

Effective dietary patterns supported by evidence:
- **Mediterranean diet** (high in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, legumes, nuts, olive oil, fish; moderate dairy and wine; low red meat and sweets)
- **DASH diet** (Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension) — emphasizes fruits, vegetables, low-fat dairy, whole grains; limits sodium, sweets, red meat
- **Reduced saturated fat and increased polyunsaturated/monounsaturated fats**
- **Reduced sodium intake**

## Physical Activity Recommendations

Aerobic activity recommendations align with the 2018 Physical Activity Guidelines for Americans:
- 150-300 minutes per week of moderate-intensity aerobic activity, OR
- 75-150 minutes per week of vigorous-intensity aerobic activity, OR
- An equivalent combination

Plus:
- Muscle-strengthening activities involving all major muscle groups on 2 or more days per week

## Effectiveness

Behavioral counseling interventions in this population produced small but clinically meaningful improvements in:
- Blood pressure
- LDL cholesterol
- Fasting glucose
- Diabetes incidence
- Adiposity (weight, BMI, waist circumference)
- Self-reported physical activity and dietary intake

- Behavioral interventions for obesity in adults
- Statin use for primary prevention of CVD
- Screening for hypertension in adults
- Screening for prediabetes and type 2 diabetes
