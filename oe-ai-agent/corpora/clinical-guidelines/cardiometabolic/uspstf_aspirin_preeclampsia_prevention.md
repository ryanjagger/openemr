# Aspirin Use to Prevent Preeclampsia: Preventive Medication

## Recommendation Summary

| Population | Recommendation | Grade |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Pregnant persons at high risk for preeclampsia | Use of low-dose aspirin (81 mg/day) as preventive medication after 12 weeks of gestation. | B |

## Implementation

- **Dose:** 81 mg/day of aspirin
- **Timing:** Begin after 12 weeks of gestation (most commonly between 12 and 28 weeks)
- **Continue through:** Delivery

## Identifying Pregnant Persons at High Risk

A pregnant person should be considered at high risk for preeclampsia if they have ONE OR MORE of these risk factors:
- Previous pregnancy with preeclampsia (especially with adverse outcome)
- Multifetal pregnancy
- Chronic hypertension
- Type 1 or type 2 diabetes
- Renal disease
- Autoimmune disease (systemic lupus erythematosus, antiphospholipid syndrome)

A pregnant person may also be considered at increased risk if they have multiple moderate risk factors:
- Nulliparity
- Obesity (BMI >30)
- Family history of preeclampsia (mother or sister)
- Sociodemographic characteristics (Black race, low socioeconomic status)
- Age 35 years or older
- Personal history factors (e.g., low birthweight or small-for-gestational-age previous infant, previous adverse pregnancy outcome, interpregnancy interval >10 years)

## Effectiveness

Low-dose aspirin in high-risk pregnant persons has been shown to:
- Reduce preeclampsia risk by approximately 15-30%
- Reduce preterm birth by approximately 14-20%
- Reduce intrauterine growth restriction
- Reduce perinatal mortality

## Safety

Low-dose aspirin during pregnancy after 12 weeks gestation has not been shown to increase risks of:
- Maternal hemorrhage
- Placental abruption
- Postpartum hemorrhage

## Importance

Preeclampsia is a serious complication of pregnancy that can lead to:
- Maternal morbidity (organ damage, eclampsia, stroke)
- Maternal mortality
- Preterm birth
- Intrauterine growth restriction
- Stillbirth
- Neonatal morbidity and mortality

Preeclampsia accounts for a significant proportion of maternal deaths in the US.
